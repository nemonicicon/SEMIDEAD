That’s a really common apartment issue—and it’s not just about frequency. What you’re seeing is usually a mix of urgency + learned habit + unclear boundaries.
Right now your dog has likely learned: “inside the building = kinda okay to go if needed.” Once that clicks, just adding more walks doesn’t automatically fix it.
What’s probably going on


The trip outside is too long (6 floors + hallways), so when the urge hits, they go at the first “not-home” spot


Scent memory: if they’ve gone in the hallway/stairs before, those spots now smell like a bathroom


They don’t fully understand that only outside is the right place



How to fix it (this works, but you have to be consistent)
1. Make the hallway “not a bathroom”


Clean any accident spots with an enzymatic cleaner (this is huge)


Regular cleaners don’t remove the smell to a dog



2. Change how you exit the building
Right now, your dog has too much freedom on the way out.
Try this:


Leash up BEFORE leaving your apartment


Keep them very close (short leash, no wandering/sniffing)


Move with purpose—don’t let them stop in the hallway/stairs


If needed, carry them until you’re outside (especially if they’re small)


This prevents them from even considering those spots.

3. Reward like crazy outside
The moment they go outside:


Immediate praise


High-value treat (something better than usual)


You want: outside = jackpot

4. Watch for “about to go” signals
If you catch even a hint (sniffing, circling, slowing down):


Interrupt quickly (“let’s go!”)


Move fast to outside


Timing matters a lot here.

5. Tighten supervision indoors
If accidents are happening before you even leave:


Limit free roaming


Use a crate or keep them near you


Take them out before they get desperate



6. Consider urgency vs. habit
You said:

“maybe he has to go so bad he can’t get outside?”

That can be part of it—but since increasing frequency didn’t fix it, habit is likely the bigger issue now.

One honest note
If a dog has rehearsed this behavior multiple times, it won’t disappear overnight. Expect a couple weeks of really consistent handling before it clicks.
